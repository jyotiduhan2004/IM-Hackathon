# Inside IndiaMART's Engineering Engine: An AI-First B2B Marketplace Bets on Agents, Automation, and Audit Trails

*Profile draft based on internal wiki review, 13 April 2026. All citations refer to pages under `wiki/`.*

## 1. The Company in Three Sentences

IndiaMART is an Indian B2B marketplace (corporate name "IndiaMART InterMESH Ltd.", per `entities/mohak-saxena.md`) that connects buyers and sellers across hundreds of product categories — from modular kitchens to industrial excavators (see the MCAT group table in `topics/whitelist-previously-negative-marked-mcats-in-google-ads.md`). Its business runs on a pipeline of "BuyLeads" (BLs) created when buyers post requirements, routed through a WhatsApp-first customer-messaging stack (`systems/whatsapp9696.md`, `systems/whatsapp.md`) and a CRM called LEAP (`systems/leap.md`). The platform is multilingual — it maintains a dedicated Hindi site covering 26 product groups as of April 2026 (`topics/hindi-im-translation-expansion.md`) — and monetises through paid seller subscriptions, BuyLead purchases, and Google Ads participation for product feeds (`topics/whitelist-previously-negative-marked-mcats-in-google-ads.md`).

## 2. Tech Priorities — Where the Investment is Going

Across ~96 topic pages, three lanes of engineering investment stand out, all reinforced by traffic on the `marketplacelaunch@indiamart.com` mailing list (`systems/marketplace-launch.md`).

**A. Agentic AI on WhatsApp and for the buyer journey.** The most heavily-referenced platform is WhatsApp9696, an LLM-driven "Agentic Buyer Chatbot" now running at "10% of repeat users" with "Intent Classification Accuracy: 93%" and a stated target latency of "below 5 seconds, ultimately 2-3 seconds" (`topics/whatsapp9696-agentic-buyer-chatbot.md`). Adjacent projects extend the same playbook: a Buyer Bot Playground & Prompt Builder that lets PMs modify system prompts in real time without engineering (`topics/buyer-bot-playground-prompt-builder.md`), a Context-Aware Pricing Framework inside WhatsApp9696, and a "Complaint Agent v2" for paid sellers on WhatsApp 9696 (index listing, `topics/complaint-agent-v2-whatsapp-9696.md`).

**B. Agents applied to internal engineering and operations.** IndiaMART is pointing LLMs at its own dev loop: PR-Agent for AI code review targeting "~145 repositories" (`topics/ai-powered-code-review-upload-api-scm-pipeline.md`); an AI-Powered Gladmin SOA BRD Agent that uses "anthropic/claude-sonnet-4" as default to draft business requirement docs (`topics/ai-powered-gladmin-soa-brd-agent.md`); a Cursor-authored GLAdmin E2E Runner that generates Playwright tests (`topics/gladmin-e2e-runner.md`); and CrashAgent, an "Autonomous Android crash fixing agent that automatically detects and fixes Android crashes using Claude Code CLI," scheduled every weekday at 09:00 IST (`topics/crashagent.md`). The Proxylysis AI Agent, Walkie-Talkie Compliance Automation, and Categorization Auditor extend this to trust, safety, and listing quality.

**C. Measurement, quality, and "audit as a skill."** There is a visible push to replace intuition with measured signal. The DSPy + GEPA Automated Speaker Labelling Pipeline frames the shift explicitly — "from intuition-driven manual prompt writing to data-driven, measurable optimization" — and reports "~95% accuracy" (`topics/dspy-gepa-automated-speaker-labeling.md`). A new SonarQube custom Quality Profile filters "millions of low-value, repetitive, and non-actionable findings" to recover signal (`topics/sonarqube-quality-profile-transformation.md`). An "Audit as a Skill" self-serve playground operationalises LLM-as-a-Judge for PMs (`topics/audit-as-a-skill.md`).

## 4. A Deeper Look: DSPy + GEPA and the "Intuition to Data" Turn

The most genuinely interesting initiative in the wiki is the DSPy + GEPA Automated Speaker Labelling Pipeline (`topics/dspy-gepa-automated-speaker-labeling.md`). For a B2B marketplace that historically grew on sales-team muscle, it is a quietly radical piece of work.

The problem is mundane: IndiaMART processes a lot of voice calls between buyers, sellers, and its own "Indiamart Executive" agents, and it was transcribing them with manually written LLM prompts that assigned each line of dialogue to a speaker role. Those prompts drifted, broke on edge cases, and had to be rewritten by hand. IndiaMART replaced that with the DSPy framework plus GEPA, treating prompts as "learned programs, automatically optimized against labelled data." The page frames it as a philosophical shift: prompts "evolve based on accuracy metrics, not human guesswork" and the process is "completely model-agnostic (not locked to any LLM)."

The numbers, broken out by call type and duration in a table on the page, show accuracy jumping from a roughly 75% baseline to 95%+ across almost every cell — PNS calls under one minute hit 98.33%, Customer-to-Customer calls under one minute hit 100%. The interesting architectural detail: they use "Gemini 2.5 Flash" for production inference but "Gemini 2.5 Pro" for the optimisation loop, taking advantage of a more expensive model up-front to produce cheaper inference forever after. Dinesh Agarwal's endorsement is unusually warm for him — "Thanks for initiating this. Very important to migrate towards this" — and Neeraj Agrawal is already trying to port the approach to classifier optimisation. If the company follows through on the stated roadmap to make DSPy+GEPA the "default framework for LLM-driven problems at IndiaMART," it is a genuinely interesting engineering-culture move for an Indian B2B company.

## 5. What's Missing from the Record

A journalist reading this wiki would walk away with plenty of material and a long list of follow-ups.

- **No financials, no user counts, no MAU, no GMV.** The wiki talks about "2-2.5 lakh images per day" moderated (`topics/central-smart-orchestrator-api.md`), "~140 BLs inserted" in 24 hours on a LEAP POC (`topics/leap-mapping-agent-used-buyleads.md`), and "70K BLS in March" for 346 whitelisted MCATs, but nothing about total marketplace volume, revenue, or seller counts. An interview is needed to anchor scale.
- **No org chart.** The wiki has 307 entity pages but almost no titles. "Leadership" is used generically; "Team member at IndiaMART InterMESH Ltd." is the most common role description (`entities/pratik-ahuja.md`, `entities/prachi-jain.md`). Who reports to whom, who owns a P&L, and which of the Agarwal-named individuals are actually family/founders (Dinesh Agarwal and Amit Agarwal both appear prominently but are not identified as founders on their pages) is invisible.
- **Strategy vs. feature-ship.** Topic pages read like launch memos. There is no competitive framing, no market narrative, no discussion of how IndiaMART positions against Alibaba, TradeIndia, or JustDial.
- **Failures and trade-offs are under-documented.** The wiki does surface real issues — Proxylysis has known false-positive risk, the LEAP Job-Seeker blocker had "BLs deleted incorrectly" and "Technical Leaks in WA81/96 and VANI" (`topics/leap-launch-blocking-job-seeker-buyleads.md`), WhatsApp9696 has 11s p95 latency. But the post-mortem/root-cause layer is thin.
- **Security and compliance are surface-level.** CrashAgent's guardrails page is the most detailed policy artefact. There is no privacy policy, no data-handling framework, no RBAC model — only scattered references like "PII Masking on SEO APIs" and "Export PII Masking."
- **Customer voice is absent.** Buyer CSAT scores, NPS, seller satisfaction, or even anecdotes are missing.

## 6. Editorial Feedback to the Internal Comms Team

**Where the wiki is strong.** Launch pages are consistently structured: Overview → Problem Statement → Implementation → Metrics → Team. That template is working hard — any reader can skim and extract what changed, who shipped it, and what the measurable outcome was. The pages on DSPy+GEPA, Central Smart Orchestrator API, Component as a Service, and SonarQube Quality Profile Transformation are standout artefacts; they would read well even as external engineering-blog posts. The topic/entity/system wikilink graph ("Related" sections) mostly works and is useful for readers moving laterally.

**Where it is noise.** (1) Entity pages are heavily duplicated and cluttered — `entities/neeraj-agrawal.md` has a trailing "Related" list where `[[chittresh-lohani]]` and `[[whatsapp9696]]` appear three times each, and fragments of text repeat; the bottom 30 lines look like a copy-paste artefact from an automated compile. (2) The compilation timestamps in frontmatter reveal this is an AI-generated wiki ("updated_by: z-ai/glm-4.6" and "Cleanup: 18 broken pages deleted (frontmatter corruption from agent edit_file)" — `wiki/log.md`). That provenance should probably be surfaced to readers rather than buried in a log. (3) Near-duplicates exist: `topics/dspy-gepa-automated-speaker-labeling.md` vs `topics/dspy-gepa-automated-speaker-labelling-pipeline.md`, `entities/arjun-gaur.md` vs `entities/arjun-gaur-clean.md`, `entities/saurabh-gupta.md` vs `entities/saurabh-gupta-clean.md`. Pick a canonical page per subject and redirect the rest. (4) Many entity pages are thin stubs with just an email and one meeting recipient flag — those add noise without signal and would be better as a directory table than individual pages. (5) `topics/sonarqube-quality-profile-transformation.md` has a broken frontmatter with two `last_compiled` keys and a split `sources` list — the YAML is invalid.

**What would make it better for a reporter.** Add a one-sentence "what this is" line at the top of every entity page. Add a single canonical "about the company" page with founding, scale, business model — none exists today. Tag topics with product-line labels (Buyer experience / Seller experience / Platform / AI infra) so the narrative lanes are visible without clicking through 96 pages.
