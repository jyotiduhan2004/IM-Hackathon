## Correlation observation

**Same-direction? No — they move in opposite directions.** Pearson r = **-0.758** on the 5-page sample. The page the heuristic ranked **lowest** (`soi-product-gst-screen-ab-test`, 6.4) was the page the judge ranked **highest** (7.67). The two top-scoring pages by heuristic (`business-credentials-filter-search-api` 8.6, `supply-chain-security-platform` 8.4) both judged at exactly 7.00.

Interpretation: the heuristic scorer is rewarding things that don't track human-style perceived quality. Looking at the per-rubric column, the heuristic's `summary_currency` and `structural_smells` columns vary widely (1–10) but the `concept_shape` and `source_density` columns are nearly always 10 — those two are doing little work to discriminate, and the smells column appears to penalise pages the judge actually liked. `soi-product-gst-screen-ab-test` lost 9 points to `summary_currency=1` (status field says `active` while body says `Completed and rolled out`) — the judge noticed the same contradiction but weighted it lightly because the page's experiment design, impact tables, and decision rationale were strong.

This is **not enough to call the heuristic broken** (n=5, single run), but it is enough to say the two scorers are not measuring the same thing, and the heuristic's high-confidence rankings (8.6 vs 6.4) do **not** survive judge review.
