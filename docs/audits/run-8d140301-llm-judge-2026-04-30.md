## F18 temporal-staleness — REPRODUCED, dominant complaint

This was last round's #1 weakness pattern. **It is still #1, and arguably worse**: every single page in this sample (except the catastrophic one) carries at least one judge complaint about open items past their target date with no resolution recorded. Examples:

- `category-auditor-direct-fenq-bl-mapping` PM: *"Timeout EDD of 2026-02-26 and deep-dive target of 2026-03-03 are both in the past relative to last_compiled (2026-04-30) — page feels stale on open items with no resolution noted."*
- `pc-item-ml-audit-table-restructuring` PM: *"Open question about monitoring DRI has a target date of 2026-02-28 that is now months in the past — page shows no resolution, making it look stale even though status is 'active'."*
- `export-indiamart-com` Newbie: *"Sources list in frontmatter covers only through January 2026 but the monthly report reference (msg-e669d793) is from 2026-02-06 and the last_compiled is 2026-04-30 — nearly three months of activity are unaccounted for with no explanation."*
- `preferred-tov-expansion-beyond-retail-ni` Newbie: *"Status of the ₹3K threshold proposal as of last compile (2026-04-30) is unknown; the open question was raised 2026-02-09 but there's no indication whether sign-off happened in the ~2.5 months since."*
- `single-bfd-enrichment-post-buyflow` PM: *"No stage/phase field (e.g., 'reverted / redesign in progress') — status: active in frontmatter is misleading since the feature is not live."*
- `search-context-bl-enquiry-forms` PM: *"Status is 'active' but the most recent source thread is from 2026-01-27 and the open items reference a 2026-04-15 target — it is now 2026-04-30 and there is no update on whether the CSL fetch migration shipped or slipped."*

**The pattern has solidified into a single recurring failure**: pages get compiled, last_compiled stamps move forward, but open-question target dates never get re-evaluated. The compiler isn't *sweeping* old open items when a new email lands on the same page. v12-U2 was supposed to address this; it hasn't shipped or hasn't reached this run. **Status: REPRODUCED (every page, every persona).**

## Page-archetype patterns — single-revision is the new bottleneck

Two new failure modes surfaced at n=10:

1. **Catastrophic compile-skip** (`dynamic-district-visibility-on-bl-card`): empty `{}` frontmatter, empty `## Related`, no source list, only a "Why it matters" paragraph. All three personas scored 1. This is not a wiki-quality issue — it's a compile-pipeline bug that produced a malformed page. **One page in 39 = a ~2.5% catastrophic-failure rate** on this run.
2. **Single-revision leaf-node pages** (`pc-item-ml-audit-table-restructuring`): correctly compiled, complete frontmatter, correct citation, but compiled from a single source thread and has only one outbound wikilink. IA gave it a 4 — the lowest non-catastrophic IA score in the sample. **The compiler is not seeding cross-links during single-source compiles**; the page is technically correct but graph-isolated.

Both failures map to **agent didn't reach for `resolve_page` early enough** — the first page didn't get any kind of structured compile, the second got a one-source compile with no neighbour-resolution step. v12 wiki-quality work should target the *first compile* of a topic, not the steady-state.
